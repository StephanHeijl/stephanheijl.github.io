Overview

Learning to deliver value with data

The Master of Applied Data Science program at HAN emphasized what many academic programs miss: the gap between building a model and deploying a solution that actually works in production. Every project required not just technical implementation, but clear communication of results to non-technical stakeholders.

This practical orientation aligned perfectly with my approach to data science. The program reinforced that the best model is worthless if it doesn't solve a real business problem or can't be maintained by the team that inherits it.

"The goal isn't to use the most sophisticated algorithm‚Äîit's to deliver the most value with the simplest solution that works."

Impact

How this shapes my work today

The emphasis on applied problem-solving directly influences how I approach projects today. Before touching data, I ask: What decision are we trying to improve? What does success look like? Who needs to understand the results?

The program also instilled a healthy skepticism of complexity for its own sake. The best data scientists I know‚Äîand the approach I try to embody‚Äîfavor interpretable models, clear documentation, and solutions that can be maintained by teams without specialized ML expertise.
